# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One reader. The person who built this. An educated, curious adult who is not a specialist in any one field but reads widely and wants depth without jargon and profundity without pretension. They open the app once a day, usually alone, likely in the morning or evening, on a phone. They are in pull mode, not push: they came here on purpose. They do not need to be persuaded to engage; they need the app to honour that intention and get out of the way.

## Product Purpose

A personal daily-learning app. One theme a day, six resonant facets, explored at the reader's pace. An antidote to shallow feeds: no algorithmic pressure, no streaks, no reach. The sequence is the design. Success is a habit that feels worth keeping after 30 days.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, unhurried. The app should feel like a well-edited journal that arrived in the post: considered, trustworthy, slightly serious. Three words: **literary, restrained, luminous**.

## References

Readwise Reader: the quiet editorial density, typographic restraint, and dark reading mode that prioritises the text over the chrome. That sense that the app has strong opinions about what matters and has removed everything else.

## Anti-references

Nothing specific named. Infer from the editorial charter's direction: not a comfort feed, not a museum of horrors, not a growth app. Avoid anything that reads as gamified (badges, streaks, confetti), shallow-social, or algorithmic. The Nocturnal Index direction rules out light-and-airy wellness aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **The sequence is the design.** Never surface mechanics that let the reader jump ahead, game the system, or skip the waiting. The constraint is the experience.
2. **Honour the intention.** The reader came here on purpose. Every piece of chrome that doesn't serve the content is a small betrayal. Reduce aggressively.
3. **Earn the reveal.** Facets are hidden until tapped. That hiddenness turns a small library into discovery. Treat the moment of reveal as earned, not routine.
4. **Trust over delight.** Pantheon's only real asset is the reader's trust that what they find is true and considered. Nothing decorative should undercut that credibility. Delight follows from precision, not from ornament.
5. **Restraint as a signal.** The single ember accent, the absence of badges, the faint read-dot: these are choices, not oversights. Each element present should feel deliberate; anything absent should feel right to be absent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Keyboard navigation and screen reader support required. Sufficient contrast on all text against dark backgrounds (minimum 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations (the rise, veil, and lift keyframes should be bypassed when the preference is set).
